How to Compress Images Online

Compressing your images with ANS Tools is quick, easy, and completely free. No software to install, no account to create — just upload and download.

  1. Click the upload area and select your JPG or PNG image file (up to 16MB)
  2. Use the quality slider to choose your compression level — 80% is the best balance of quality and size reduction
  3. Click "Compress Image" to process your image
  4. Preview the compressed image and compare the file size reduction
  5. Click "Download Compressed Image" to save the smaller file to your device

Why Compress Images?

Image compression is one of the most effective ways to improve website speed, reduce storage usage, and make sharing easier. Large image files are one of the biggest causes of slow websites. A slow website means lower Google rankings, higher bounce rates, and frustrated users.

Our free image compressor can reduce JPG and PNG file sizes by 50-80% without any noticeable loss in visual quality. This makes our tool perfect for web developers, bloggers, social media managers, eCommerce store owners, and anyone who needs smaller image files quickly.

Best Quality Settings for Different Use Cases

The quality slider controls how much compression is applied. Here is a guide to choosing the right quality setting:

  • 90-99% — Minimal compression, almost no quality loss. Best for professional photography where quality is critical.
  • 75-85% — The sweet spot for most images. Significant file size reduction with no visible quality loss at normal viewing sizes.
  • 50-70% — Heavier compression. Good for thumbnails, social media, and images where exact quality is less critical.
  • 10-45% — Maximum compression. Visible quality reduction but very small file sizes. Use for previews or low-priority images.

Compress Images for Websites

Website images should ideally be under 200KB for fast loading. Large background images can be up to 500KB. Images larger than 1MB will significantly slow down your page. Google PageSpeed Insights and Core Web Vitals both penalize sites with unoptimized images. Our compressor helps you pass image optimization requirements.

Compress Images for Social Media

Social media platforms like Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, and WhatsApp compress images when you upload them, often resulting in quality loss. To maintain the best quality, compress your images to the platform's recommended size before uploading and the platform will do less compression on its end.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does image compression reduce quality?

At quality settings of 75-85%, image compression produces files that look identical to the original at normal screen viewing sizes. Only very low quality settings (below 50%) produce noticeable quality differences.

What image formats are supported?

Our image compressor supports JPG (JPEG) and PNG image formats. These are the two most common image formats for web use.

Is there a file size limit?

Yes, the maximum upload size is 16MB. This is large enough to handle almost all standard photographs and graphics.

Are my images stored on your server?

No. Images are processed in memory and immediately returned to you. We do not store, save, or keep any copies of your uploaded images.

Can I compress multiple images at once?

Currently our tool processes one image at a time. Simply refresh and upload each image you need to compress.
