How is Age Calculated?

Age is calculated by finding the difference between your date of birth and today's date. The exact age considers the calendar properly — it accounts for leap years and the varying number of days in each month. For example, if you were born on February 29 (a leap year birthday), the calculator handles this correctly by using February 28 in non-leap years for your birthday.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my exact age in days?

Enter your date of birth in the calculator above and click "Calculate Age." The result will show your exact age in total days lived, accounting for leap years and calendar differences.

Is the age calculation accurate for leap year birthdays?

Yes. Our calculator correctly handles February 29 leap year birthdays by using February 28 in non-leap years for the birthday anniversary calculation.

Can I calculate someone else's age?

Yes. Simply enter any date of birth to calculate the age of any person. This is useful for parents calculating their child's age, HR professionals verifying ages, or anyone curious about a person's exact age.

What is the difference between age in years and total days lived?

Age in years tells you your age rounded down to complete years (e.g., 25 years). Total days lived counts every single day since your birth date, including the current partial year.
